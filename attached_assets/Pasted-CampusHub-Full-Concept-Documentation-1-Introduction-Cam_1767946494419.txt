CampusHub – Full Concept Documentation
1. Introduction
CampusHub is a digital platform designed to solve a critical problem faced by university and college students: finding safe, affordable, and reliable off‑campus housing. It also serves as a bridge between students, landlords, and nearby campus services.
CampusHub aims to be the go‑to housing and accommodation marketplace for both new and continuing students who are moving out of hostels or joining campus for the first time.

2. Core Problem CampusHub Solves
1.Students struggle to find legit, safe, affordable housing near campus.
2.Many landlords cannot easily reach students, leading to vacancies.
3.Freshers joining campus lack reliable guidance on where to stay.
4.Students waste time and money visiting random rooms that don’t match their preferences.
5.No centralized, trusted verification system exists to filter unsafe or low‑quality houses.

3. Target Users
1. Continuing Students
Students who want to move from hostels to private rooms.
Students looking for cheaper, better, or more private accommodation.
2. Freshers (New Students Joining Campus)
Students looking for clear, guided options for safe rooms before reporting.
3. Landlords & Property Owners
People renting single rooms, bedsitters, 1-bedroom units, etc.
Hostels or private student residences.
4. Campus Administration (Optional – Phase 3)
They can use the platform for student welfare partnerships.

4. Features of CampusHub
A. For Students
Browse available rooms near campus.
Filter by rent, location, type, amenities.
View verified listings only.
Check distance from campus.
Book viewing appointments.
Reserve a room online.
Chat with landlords.
Rate and review places.
B. For Landlords
Create an account.
Post rooms with photos, prices, amenities.
Update availability.
Receive booking or reservation requests.
Get verified through the CampusHub verification process.
C. Smart Recommendation System (Future)
Suggests rooms to students based on their preferences.
D. CampusHub Verification System
Physical inspection by CampusHub field agents.
Checks security, cleanliness, water, electricity, environment.
Only verified houses get a green badge.

5. Revenue Model – How CampusHub Earns
1. Landlord Subscription Model (Primary)
Landlords pay monthly or yearly to keep their listings active.
Example: - Basic: KES 300/month - Premium: KES 700/month (more visibility) - Pro: KES 1,500/month (top ranking + verification badge)
2. Listing Fees (Alternative)
Landlords pay per listed room or per verified badge.
3. Commission on Bookings (Optional)
CampusHub earns a small commission when a student reserves a room.
4. Ads & Partnerships
Ads from campus shops, barbers, food joints, furniture shops.
CampusHub marketplace for students buying beds, chairs, etc.
5. Verification Fee
Landlords pay a small fee for an official safety/quality inspection.

6. Value Proposition
For Students
Saves time.
Ensures safety.
No being scammed by fake landlords.
Affordable and verified housing options.
For Landlords
Guaranteed traffic from students.
Higher occupancy.
Increased trust through verification.
For Campus Administration (Optional)
Improved student welfare.
Safer housing environment.

7. CampusHub Workflow
Student Side
1.Create account.
2.Set preferences (budget, room type, distance).
3.Browse verified listings.
4.Book viewing.
5.Reserve/Chat with landlord.
6.Move in.
Landlord Side
1.Create account.
2.Submit property details.
3.Choose payment plan.
4.Request verification (optional but recommended).
5.Receive student requests.

8. Competitive Advantage
Verification badge that eliminates scams.
Built specifically for students, not general house‑hunting.
Easy interface.
Localized to each campus.
Ratings and reviews.
Digital bookings.

9. Long‑Term Expansion Ideas
Move to multiple campuses.
Add marketplace for furniture.
Add transport partners for moving services.
Add student job board.
Campus events section.
Emergency reporting hotline.

10. Conclusion
CampusHub is not just a house‑hunting app—it is a student survival tool. It gives students trust, guidance, and safe options. For landlords, it provides direct access to the student market. For admin, it boosts campus welfare.
CampusHub has strong potential to become a nationwide student housing ecosystem.
